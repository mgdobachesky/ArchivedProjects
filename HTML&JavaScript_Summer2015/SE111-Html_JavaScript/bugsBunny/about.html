The carrot-munching star of Warner Brothers cartoons, Bugs Bunny was the most enduring American cartoon character of the 20th century. The star of the Warner Brothers cartoons, Bugs was a cocky, wisecracking, good-hearted hare who battled Elmer Fudd, Daffy Duck and other nemeses in dozens of animated short films. Bugs' debut as a star was the 1940 short A Wild Hare, where he first uttered his trademark line, "What's up, Doc?"
